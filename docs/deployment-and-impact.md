# Deployment Model & Impact Analysis

## The Gender Argument — Made Structural

Most "gender-focused" water solutions add a pink UI and call it empowerment. JalSakhi makes women the **critical infrastructure** of India's water quality system.

---

## Deployment Architecture

### Level 1: Self-Help Group (SHG)

India's National Rural Livelihoods Mission (NRLM / DAY-NRLM) has organized:
- **12 million SHGs** across India
- **~140 million women members**
- Present in **99% of blocks** and **90% of Gram Panchayats**
- Already have: bank accounts, smartphones, training infrastructure, government linkages

**JalSakhi leverages this EXISTING infrastructure.**

### Level 2: Jal Sakhi (Water Guardian)

Each SHG designates 2-3 members as Jal Sakhis:
- Receive 2-day training on water testing protocol
- Equipped with JalSakhi kit (potentiostat + electrodes + strips)
- Test community water sources on a weekly schedule
- Earn INR 10-20 per validated test (micro-payment via UPI)
- Become recognized water quality monitors in their village

**This creates a livelihood opportunity, not just volunteer work.**

### Level 3: Gram Panchayat Water Quality Register

- All test data feeds into a village-level water quality register
- Gram Panchayat can see which sources are safe, which need attention
- Links to Paani Samiti (Village Water & Sanitation Committee)
- Enables evidence-based water infrastructure decisions

### Level 3: Block/District Intelligence

- Aggregated data from all SHGs in a block/district
- Contamination heatmaps at administrative boundaries
- Integration with IMIS (Integrated Management Information System) of Jal Jeevan Mission
- District Water Quality Officers get real-time dashboards

## Deployment Phases

### Phase 1: Proof of Concept (3 months)
- **Location**: 1 Gram Panchayat in a known ammonia-affected district
  - Recommended: Bhagalpur (Bihar), Unnao (UP), or Guntur (AP) — all have documented ammonia contamination
- **Scale**: 5 SHGs, 15 Jal Sakhis
- **Targets**: 30 water sources tested weekly, 360 test data points
- **Budget**: INR 2 lakh ($2,500)
- **Success metric**: Correlation between JalSakhi readings and NABL lab verification > 90%

### Phase 2: District Pilot (6 months)
- **Scale**: 100 SHGs across 3 blocks
- **Coverage**: ~500 water sources
- **Integration**: Connect to District Water Quality Testing Lab
- **Budget**: INR 10 lakh ($12,000)
- **Partnerships**: NRLM state office, District Jal Jeevan Mission office

### Phase 3: State Scale (12 months)
- **Scale**: 5,000 SHGs across 1 state
- **Manufacturing**: Local SPE production (screen printing facility)
- **Revenue model activated**: Municipal dashboard subscriptions begin
- **Budget**: INR 1 crore ($120,000) — mostly SPE manufacturing setup

## Impact Quantification

### Direct Impact: Health

**Problem**: Ammonia in groundwater causes:
- Infant methemoglobinemia ("blue baby syndrome")
- Liver and kidney stress
- Gastrointestinal illness

**JalSakhi impact**: If 1,000 Jal Sakhis test 10,000 sources monthly:
- Estimated 15-20% sources found with ammonia > WHO limit (based on CGWB data)
- ~5,000 families alerted to unsafe water they were unknowingly drinking
- Estimated 200-500 hospitalizations prevented annually (based on waterborne disease burden data)

### Direct Impact: Time

**Problem**: Rural women spend 1.4 billion hours/year collecting water in India.

**JalSakhi impact**:
- Contamination maps show which sources are safe
- Routing optimization: average 15 min reduction in collection time per trip
- For 5,000 households: **450,000 hours saved annually**
- Equivalent to **INR 2.25 crore** in women's time value (at INR 50/hr)

### Direct Impact: Income

**For Jal Sakhis**:
- 10 tests/week × INR 15/test = INR 600/month supplementary income
- For 1,000 Jal Sakhis = INR 72 lakh annually into women's hands
- Skills development in STEM, data collection, community health

### Systemic Impact: Governance

**For Jal Jeevan Mission**:
- Real-time ground truth on water quality (vs. annual lab surveys)
- Evidence for infrastructure investment (where to build treatment plants)
- Compliance monitoring of existing water supply systems
- Early warning for contamination events (hours vs weeks)

## Why Women SHGs and NOT:

### Why not ASHA workers?
- ASHAs are overloaded (health surveys, immunization, maternal care)
- No spare bandwidth for water testing
- SHG women have more flexibility and financial motivation

### Why not Gram Panchayat employees?
- Limited in number (Sarpanch + few members)
- Often lack smartphone access
- Political dynamics affect data reliability
- SHGs are more trusted at household level

### Why not NGOs?
- Not scalable (depend on donor funding)
- Not sustainable (project-based, time-limited)
- SHGs are permanent community institutions

### Why not government labs?
- Only 2,200 NABL labs for 640,000 villages
- Average testing: 1 test per village per year
- JalSakhi enables 52 tests per source per year (weekly)
- **52x improvement in monitoring frequency**

### Revenue Streams

1. **SPE consumables** — recurring revenue from electrode sales
   - Margin: 60-70% at scale
   - Revenue per SHG per year: INR 1,500

2. **Municipal dashboard subscriptions** — SaaS model
   - INR 50,000/month per district
   - Includes: heatmaps, alerts, reports, API access
   - 100 districts = INR 6 crore/year

3. **Data licensing** — anonymized water quality data
   - Useful for: research, insurance, real estate, public health
   - Premium API access for enterprises

4. **Government contracts** — Jal Jeevan Mission monitoring
   - Under SBMG (Swachh Bharat Mission Gramin) or JJM
   - Per-village monitoring contracts

### Unit Economics at Scale

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Cost per test | INR 25 |
| Lab test equivalent value | INR 500-2000 |
| Cost saving per test | 80-95% |
| Tests per SHG per month | 40 |
| Revenue per SHG per month | INR 175 (consumables) |
| Revenue per district per month | INR 50,000 (dashboard) |
| Break-even | ~200 SHGs deployed |

## Alignment with Government Programs

### Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM)
- Target: Functional household tap connections to every rural household
- **Gap**: No continuous quality monitoring after tap is installed
- **JalSakhi fills**: Ongoing quality verification by community women

### Swachh Bharat Mission — Gramin (SBM-G)
- Phase 2 focuses on ODF Plus: solid + liquid waste management
- Water quality is a key component
- JalSakhi data supports SBM-G ODF Plus monitoring

### NRLM / DAY-NRLM
- Existing SHG infrastructure for deployment
- Livelihood diversification for women (Jal Sakhi as a new livelihood)
- Digital literacy programs already cover smartphone usage

### Atal Bhujal Yojana (ABY)
- Groundwater management in 7 states
- Community participation in groundwater monitoring
- JalSakhi provides the measurement tool
