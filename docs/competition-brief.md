# Competition Brief

## Event: World Water Day 2026 Challenge

### Theme: "Water and Gender"

### Overview
"Water is Life!" and access to safe water is essential for healthy communities. In many parts of the world, women and girls are primarily responsible for collecting and managing water. Limited access to safe water and sanitation affects their health, education, and opportunities.

The theme of World Water Day 2026 - "Water and Gender" highlights the need to address these inequalities. Improving equitable access to water can empower women and strengthen communities. Ensuring inclusive and sustainable water solutions is vital for a better future.

### Problem Statement Categories

1. **Mitigations/offset strategies for Ammonia in drinking water**
   - Ammonia contamination is a growing concern in drinking water
   - Sources: agricultural runoff, industrial waste, sewage
   - WHO guideline: 0.5 mg/L maximum
   - Need cost-effective detection and mitigation

2. **Technologies for Water Neutrality in household**
   - Water neutrality = consuming only as much water as you return/offset
   - Household-level solutions for water conservation
   - Greywater recycling, rainwater harvesting
   - Tracking and gamification of water usage

3. **Integrating smart technologies in water distribution and carrier systems**
   - IoT-based monitoring of water pipelines
   - Leak detection and pressure management
   - Smart routing for water collection (relevant to women carrying water)
   - Real-time supply chain visibility

4. **Affordable household water quality sensing and filtration**
   - Low-cost sensors for key water quality parameters
   - Point-of-use filtration systems
   - Accessible to rural and low-income households
   - Easy to use without technical knowledge

5. **AI Driven Water management**
   - Machine learning for water quality prediction
   - Demand forecasting and supply optimization
   - Anomaly detection for contamination events
   - Data-driven decision making for water infrastructure

### Key Evaluation Criteria (Expected)
- Innovation and originality
- Technical feasibility
- Impact on gender equity / women empowerment
- Scalability and sustainability
- Cost-effectiveness
- Presentation quality

### Our Strategy
- **Combine categories 4 + 5** as primary focus
- **Integrate elements** from categories 1, 2, and 3
- **Emphasize the gender angle** throughout the solution
- **Build a working prototype** to demonstrate feasibility
- **Keep costs low** to show real-world applicability
